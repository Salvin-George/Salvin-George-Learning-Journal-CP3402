## Learning Activities & Resources
- This week I conducted a learning experiment to compare two study techniques: passive revision (re-reading notes) and active recall (retrieving information from memory without notes). I tested both methods using lecture content from this subject and compared how well I could retain and recall information immediately after studying and again after a delay.

- Our group also practiced our presentation for the group project and helped develop a live demo of the workflow.

## Resources used:
- Lecture slides and tutorial materials from this subject
- Self-made / AI practice questions based on lecture content

## Estimated Hours
- About 4 hours total over 4 days (Learning experiment)
- About 3h for the Practice Presentation


## Content Insights

- I found that passive study (re-reading notes) made the content feel familiar, but did not guarantee that I could actually recall it later without support. This was the case for exam style questions or applied questions from my other subjects. 

- After testing both methods, I noticed that I made fewer mistakes and had better long-term memory and understanding  when using active recall compared to passive revision.

- From the group presentation rehearsal I discovered a couple of areas of where we were missing information about the workflow and how to better structure the slide structure to ensure that slides and overall flow of the presentation remained consistent and flowed from one person to another.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights

From a learning perspective, this experiment helped me understand that the effectiveness of studying is highly dependent on the method used, not just the time spent.

This week also highlighted that my usual study habit of re-reading notes is not the most efficient approach for long-term learning. Moving forward, I should use active recall methods such as self-testing and summarising from memory. Overall, this helped me understand how to better help and improve my studying and how time spent on studying did not directly increase my understanding of the material.
